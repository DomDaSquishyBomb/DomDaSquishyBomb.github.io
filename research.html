Research

by Dominic Le

About

During my time as an undergraduate (ongoing and strong), I have had a few opportunities to help with research and lead some independent projects to explore my interests in the cognitive sciences. While my knowledge base significantly grows with every course I endure, the interdisciplinary nature of cognitive science is very fitting to how I like to explore each topic of interest. The topics that interest me the most right now are causal reasoning, counterfactual thinking, cognitive development, and cognitive architectures .

Specifically, I am curious about what underlying structure could represent atomic objects and relationships when we reason about them? How do children translate everyday language into causal models to explain and predict events? Why do alternative and fictional worlds seem so intuitive, and what does that say about our cogware?
