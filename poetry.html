The Plan

April 15 2024

The guardian, spirit, and person: what happened?
The three years it takes to lose everything,
the 15 years of holding on to anything that looked like
what you saw when you were in my position 30 years ago.
I want a family of three and 90 thousand dollars a year;
I'll have three favourite colours and three virtues I pass on to my kids;
I'll give them simple digestible apt sentences;
They'll grow up to be rich, to be old, and they won't need to take care of me.
I will deduct from my conduct three ways my parents mistreated me,
And I pass it off to the next family tree.
I want nothing to do with my future, and I will accept all disease.
You will be named three weeks before you are born,
and until you are 18, I will lose 1/6 of myself every 3 years.

(as read for Toronto Poetry Society)

Failiure Set into Inspiration for Celebrate!

April 15 2024

Seething is a piece of your blinding teeth,
sliding flyers into pockets, activist creep.
Recycle ideas from your failure set.
Feel good; feel sad that no one else took care of your pet,
I can't think of anything worse to forget.
Failure set is the only set that makes no sense.

Again,

Capitalism overvalues beliefs
Which automates the precedence of actions.
Distance from parameters allows us to view them as categorically null.
The only one responsible is the one casting blame.
Of course they'd never win democratically; they're the most special person you've ever
met.

Whiff

April 15 2024

Get a whiff of this, suck sharply.
It's surfacing, dripping down the boundary of your toughness and your close-less < considerations.
Can I count this even if you never ended happy?
Can we count this even if we never ended?
Sadly, by the fern, the stiffness in your throat with unhurt ego only in shouting.
Was the first, now the third; the gull to be exhausted sunshine's cooldown.
Idiot! What if you sold my clothes or gifted your negativeness.
If you watered your plants, your pets, every precious mayby.
With the last throw; we are close now when sometimes you get to thinking,
Please, from forever yours, can we have together dreams again?

(as read for Toronto Poetry Society)
